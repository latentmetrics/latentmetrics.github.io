Biography

Bong-Sik Kim is a mathematician whose research sits at the boundary of classical analysis and modern Scientific Machine Learning. He holds a Ph.D. in Mathematics from Arizona State University and has more than fifteen years of experience in UAE higher education.

His research focuses on the mathematical foundations of Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) — specifically on the question of when and why these models can be trusted to faithfully encode physical law. This requires a combination of tools: functional analysis and Sobolev theory for regularity, Leray-type regularization theory for understanding smoothed PDE solutions, numerical analysis for error bounds, and operator learning for parametric families of PDEs.

A recent peer-reviewed article in Results in Applied Mathematics (2025) represents an early output of this program. Current work is developing a comprehensive error analysis and numerical validation framework for PINNs applied to the 1D Leray-Burgers equation.

On the educational side, Kim is authoring Discrete Thinking: Networks, Logic, and AI with Python, a mathematically rigorous undergraduate textbook that integrates discrete mathematics, graph theory, logic, and foundational AI with working Python implementations applied to real datasets. His teaching across fifteen years at the university level reflects a consistent commitment to building genuine mathematical competence alongside computational fluency.

Latent Metrics Institute was founded to provide an independent structure for this research and educational work, and to offer mathematical consulting for teams seeking rigor in machine learning system design.
